Bessel Helmert Tab

The Bessel-Helmert formulas are used to calculate distances and directions on the ellipsoid.

Geodesics and Azimuths

Ellipsoidal geometry is used. In this geometry "straight lines" are the geodetic lines/geodesics, i.e. the shortest line from one point to another on the ellipsoid. Thus, 'angle' refers to angles between crossing geodesics and 'direction' to the directions of geodesics (see below).
From each point (except the north pole) on the ellipsoid the geodesic to the north pole is used as reference for other geodesics passing through that point: The direction of a geodesic in a point is called an azimuth and is measured as the angle between the reference direction (towards north) and the geodesic.

When two points (Stn1 and Stn2) on the ellipsoid are connected by a geodesic, the azimuths of the geodesic connecting the points are called the "Forward" and the "Backward" azimuth respectively. The forward azimuth is measured in Stn1 as the azimuth towards Stn2. The backward azimuth is the azimuth of the geodesic towards Stn1.

Modes

For a setup of two points connected by a geodesic, you can use this widget to calculate unknown elements from given (elements).
The dialogue has two modes, each corresponding to the calculation type:
  1. Based on coordinates to two points, calculate the ellipsoidal distance between the points and the forward and backward azimuths.
  2. From distance and direction (forward azimuth) from one coordinated point, calculate the coordinates of the other point.
Radio buttons in the lower left corner are used to toggle the mode selection.

Coordinate systems

In both modes, you have to select the input system which the coordinates of the two stations refer to - ellipsoid parameters are taken from this system. The geometric calculations (distance and azimuth) will be performed on the corresponding ellipsoid.
It is also possible to define a custom ellipsoid by specifying major axis and flattening. The coordinate system will always be geographic in this case.
